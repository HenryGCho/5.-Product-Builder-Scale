Capability 01

Orchestration Fluency agent-native

Designing, directing and improving agent and human workflows so that delivery is reliable, repeatable, and continuously improving.

Workflow design Structuring end-to-end processes where agents and humans each have defined roles and handoff points.
AI tooling Selecting, configuring, and maintaining the agent tools that power delivery workflows.
Process orchestration Coordinating multiple workflows and agents into a coherent delivery system.
L1 Absent
Uses AI tools individually and ad hoc — each task is a one-off prompt with no connection to a broader workflow. No repeatable process exists where agents participate as consistent, designed-in contributors to delivery.
L2 Emerging Baseline
Has linked some agent tasks into sequences but workflows are fragile — they break when inputs change or context is missing. Tool selection is opportunistic rather than deliberate. Workflows are designed for convenience, not reliability or scalability.
L3 Developing Target
Has designed repeatable agent workflows for the team's most common tasks — with defined inputs, expected outputs, and human checkpoints. Can identify where a workflow is failing and diagnose the root cause. Uses a consistent, maintained toolset rather than constantly switching.
L4 Strong
Designs and maintains a suite of agent workflows that reliably accelerate delivery — each with clear scope, error handling, and human-in-the-loop decision points. Others on the team can run and adapt them without intervention; continuous improvement happens on a regular cadence.
L5 Exceptional
Has developed workflow architectures or orchestration patterns that others study and replicate. Can design a reliable human-agent delivery system for a new domain from first principles; their approach to orchestration is referenced as a model for agent-native product teams.
Capability 02

Operational Clarity

Metrics, process and systems that give the team — and agents — visibility into what's working, what's not, and what to do about it.

Metrics design Choosing leading indicators that drive real decisions, not vanity numbers.
Process documentation Writing processes clearly enough that agents and new team members can follow them without a walkthrough.
OKR execution Setting goals that are reviewed and acted on, not just set and forgotten.
L1 Absent
The team operates without shared visibility into progress, quality, or health. Metrics, if tracked at all, are output counts rather than leading indicators. Knowledge lives in people's heads — no documented processes exist that agents or new hires could work from.
L2 Emerging
Has identified key metrics and communicates progress through regular updates. Processes exist but documentation is patchy and out of date. The team knows broadly what it's doing, but visibility breaks down under pressure or when context needs to transfer to a new person or agent.
L3 Developing Baseline
Maintains a stable set of meaningful metrics that the team uses to make real decisions. Core processes are documented well enough for agents and new team members to execute without constant guidance. OKRs or equivalent goals are set, reviewed, and used to prioritise work — not just set and forgotten.
L4 Strong
The team and agents operate with consistent, shared visibility — metrics are meaningful and acted on, documented processes are maintained and current, and goal progress is reviewed regularly enough to influence decisions. Operational clarity is a deliberate investment; when things slip, the cause is diagnosed rather than worked around.
L5 Exceptional
Has developed operations frameworks, metric systems, or documentation approaches others adopt. Their approach to operational clarity — designed explicitly for a human-agent team — is referenced as a model for scaling product organisations without losing coherence.
Capability 03

Learning Velocity

Closing feedback loops fast — synthesising what agents, experiments, and customers reveal into decisions that improve the next iteration.

Experiment design Running structured tests with a hypothesis, success metric, and decision rule before you start.
Feedback loop design Building short, reliable cycles that surface signal from users, agents, and the market.
Data synthesis Combining quantitative and qualitative signals into actionable insight, not just dashboards.
L1 Absent
Learning is accidental — insights come from visible failures or customer complaints, not designed experiments. Feedback loops are long, informal, and unreliable. Agent output is used or discarded, but not treated as a signal worth analysing.
L2 Emerging
Runs occasional experiments and collects user feedback, but the process is inconsistent and insights don't reliably change what's being built. Feedback loops exist but are slow, manually maintained, and often deprioritised when delivery pressure builds.
L3 Developing Baseline
Designs and runs deliberate experiments — hypothesis, success metric, timeframe, and decision rule — and has a cadence for synthesising what's been learned back into the roadmap. Feedback loops are maintained and include signal from both users and agent output quality.
L4 Strong
Operates with continuously running experiments and short, partly-automated feedback loops that influence the next sprint. Failures stay small because the team learns before they compound. Agent outputs are treated as hypothesis-generating — quality issues are diagnosed and fed back into briefs and workflows, not just corrected.
L5 Exceptional
Has developed experiment or feedback loop designs others adopt. Their approach to learning velocity — synthesising signals from users, agents, and the market simultaneously and acting on them faster than competitors — is referenced as a model for high-performing product teams.
Capability 04

Quality Stewardship agent-native

The human taste gate — setting and maintaining the standard for what actually ships, especially when agents generate significant output.

Quality review Evaluating output against an explicit standard consistently enough that others learn the bar.
Taste calibration Developing and sharing a clear sense of what 'good' looks like so the team can self-assess.
Standards setting Making quality criteria explicit, documented, and usable by both people and agents.
L1 Absent
Quality is assessed against whether something works, not whether it's good. No explicit standards exist — what ships is shaped by whoever reviewed it last and how much time was available. Agent outputs are accepted or rejected without a clear rubric.
L2 Emerging Baseline
Has a sense of what "good" looks like for their product but hasn't made it explicit. Quality reviews happen but are subjective and inconsistent — others on the team and agents can't reliably predict what will clear the bar or why something was rejected.
L3 Developing Target
Has articulated an explicit quality standard for what the product ships — specific enough that others can apply it and agents can be briefed against it. Reviews happen on a defined cadence. Can distinguish "good enough to ship" from "not yet" with consistent reasoning that others can learn from.
L4 Strong
Maintains a living quality rubric that evolves as the product's standard rises. Agents are briefed against it and their outputs reviewed against it systematically. The team has internalised the standard — the founder's taste is no longer the only quality gate. What ships is reliably at or above bar without requiring personal review of everything.
L5 Exceptional
Has developed taste calibration frameworks or quality standards that define excellence for a category. Their approach to quality stewardship in an agent-native environment — where significant output is generated rather than authored — is studied by others. The work they ship is described by peers as setting the bar.
Capability 05

Founder State

Energy management, composure and clarity under sustained pressure — the operating system underneath everything else.

Energy management Designing rhythms and routines that sustain high performance without chronic depletion.
Resilience building Developing the capacity to recover quickly from setbacks and sustained pressure periods.
Focus practice Maintaining clarity about what matters most when everything feels urgent.
L1 Absent
Operating in a state of chronic overload — reactive, depleted, and making decisions under sustained cognitive stress. Recovery and energy management are not treated as operational concerns; they're viewed as luxuries the role doesn't allow.
L2 Emerging Baseline
Aware that energy and state affect performance, and has started developing practices — exercise, sleep, boundaries — but they're inconsistent and the first thing abandoned under pressure. Can recognise depletion after the fact but struggles to act on warning signs in time.
L3 Developing Target
Has consistent practices that maintain energy and focus under normal operating conditions. Can identify warning signs of depletion and has recovery strategies that work. Pressure periods don't derail performance for extended periods — recovery happens within days, not weeks.
L4 Strong
Operates with high, consistent energy and composure — others notice it and the team draws stability from it. Working environment and rhythms are deliberately designed around sustainability. The quality of decisions under pressure remains high; reactivity is noticed and managed, not acted on.
L5 Exceptional
Has developed an exceptional, studied relationship with their own state — and shares what they've learned in ways others adopt. Their approach to founder performance and wellbeing under sustained pressure is referenced by others in the field. Can return to high-quality judgment reliably, even through extended periods of difficulty.
