Why a new role?

With AI agents, a single founder can now operate as designer, engineer, and product person simultaneously. But this expanded role demands a new set of skills — knowing when to direct, when to delegate, and when to override. This operating system maps 15 capabilities across 3 phases — so you can see where you're strong, where the gaps are, and where to focus next.

AI-supported skills

Most capabilities in this framework aren't new — customer insight, strategic thinking, business model design have always mattered. What's changed is that AI agents now amplify these skills. A founder with strong customer insight can use agents to synthesise research faster. A clear delivery plan lets agents execute with less hand-holding. The existing 11 capabilities are AI-supported: traditional skills made more powerful with agent assistance.

New agent-native skills

Four capabilities are genuinely new — skills that didn't exist before AI agents became part of the delivery workflow: Context Architecture, Operating Model, Orchestration Fluency, and Quality Stewardship. These define how well you structure work for agents, divide responsibility between humans and AI, and maintain the quality bar as output scales. They are the emerging edge of what it means to build with agents.

How to use this

  1. Understand the phases — three stages of building: understanding your context, envisioning what to build, and delivering it.
  2. Understand the capability outcomes — each capability describes what good looks like at each level.
  3. Understand the skills — each capability breaks down into the specific skills needed to achieve that outcome.
  4. Assess yourself — rate yourself honestly against each skill to identify gaps and create a growth plan. Baselines are the level you should reach within 3 months.
